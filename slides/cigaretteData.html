Number of Cigarettes

This data was published by Housefresh in 2024.It references research from Berkeley’s Earth, a non-profit organization in the U.S. focused on environmental data science.
The data includes a rule-of-thumb comparing exposure to PM2.5 particles to cigarettes smoked: smoking one cigarette per day is roughly equivalent to a PM2.5 level of 22 µg/m3.
This data compares the level of PM2.5 pollutants in various UK regions to the harm caused by smoking,
illustrating how living in those areas is equivalent to indirectly smoking a certain number of cigarettes each year.
